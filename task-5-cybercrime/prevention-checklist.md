# Stay Safe Online: A Guide for the Literate Engineer
To protect your digital identity and financial assets, follow these 8 actionable steps:

## Account & Data Security
Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Always use 2FA (preferably via an Authenticator app rather than SMS) for your Gmail, GitHub, and LinkedIn accounts.

Use a Passphrase, Not a Password: Instead of Aklesh@123, use a long string like BlueCoffeeRailway2026!. It is much harder for brute-force attacks to crack.

Audit App Permissions: Periodically check your phone settings to see which apps have access to your Contacts, Microphone, and Location. If an app doesn't need it to function, revoke it.

Public Wi-Fi Hygiene: Never log into your bank account or college portal while connected to free public Wi-Fi (like at railway stations or cafes) without a trusted VPN.

## Financial & UPI Safety (India Specific)
The "PIN for Pay" Rule: Remember that your UPI PIN is only for sending money. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to "receive" a refund or a prize, it is a 100% scam.

Verify UPI IDs: Before hitting 'Send' on GPay or PhonePe, always verify the name of the recipient. Scammers often use names similar to official brands (e.g., electricity.bill.pay@upi instead of the official board ID).

## Digital Footprint & Browsing
Check the URL (HTTPS): Before entering credentials on any site, ensure the URL starts with https:// and look for the padlock icon. Be wary of "look-alike" domains like g00gle.com.

Avoid Pirated Software: Downloading "cracked" versions of development tools or games often installs Keyloggers that record every keystroke you make, including your passwords.

## How to Report a Cybercrime
If you or someone you know falls victim to a digital scam, immediate action is critical:

 Online Portal: Register your complaint at cybercrime.gov.in.

 National Helpline: Dial 1930 immediately to report financial fraud (the "Golden Hour" is vital for freezing the stolen funds in the scammer's account)
