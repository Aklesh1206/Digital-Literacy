# Case Study: The "Refund" QR Code Trap
## What the Crime Is:
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of financial cybercrime where attackers trick victims into authorizing a transaction that drains their bank account. Unlike traditional hacking, this relies on Social Engineering—manipulating the victim’s trust and lack of technical awareness.

## How it Typically Happens:

The Hook: The attacker finds a victim on an online marketplace (like OLX) or poses as a customer care executive. They claim they need to "send" a refund or payment for an item.

The Bait: The attacker sends a QR code via WhatsApp, telling the victim, "Scan this code to receive the money in your account."

The Trap: When the victim scans the code, their UPI app (GPay, PhonePe, or BHIM) asks for their UPI PIN.

The Execution: A "literate engineer" knows that a PIN is only required to send money, never to receive it. However, the victim enters the PIN, thinking it's a verification step.

The Theft: The moment the PIN is entered, the requested amount is instantly debited from the victim’s account and moved to the attacker’s untraceable wallet.

## Who is Typically Targeted:
Students, small-scale online sellers, and senior citizens are frequent targets. Attackers look for people who are comfortable using digital apps but may not understand the underlying transaction protocols.

## Consequences:
The primary consequence is immediate financial loss, which is often difficult to recover due to the instantaneous nature of UPI. Beyond the money, victims experience a loss of digital confidence and potential identity exposure if the attacker used a "KYC Update" pretext to gain further personal details.
