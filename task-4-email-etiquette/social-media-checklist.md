## Responsible Social Media Checklist for College Students

✅ The Do's

1. Privacy Audit: Regularly check your account privacy settings to control who can see your personal information and posts.

2. Think Before You Post: Ask yourself if you would be comfortable with a future employer or professor seeing the content.

3. Fact-Check: Verify information from reliable sources before sharing news or academic-related updates.

4. Professional Networking: Use platforms like LinkedIn to showcase your projects (like `walkthrough-ai`) and certifications.

5. Positive Engagement: Use your voice to support peers, share educational resources, and participate in constructive discussions.

---

❌ The Don'ts

1. Oversharing: Avoid posting sensitive personal details like your home address, current precise location, or private ID numbers.

2. Plagiarism: Never claim someone else's digital work, code, or creative content as your own without proper credit.

3. Engage in Toxicity: Avoid "flame wars," cyberbullying, or spreading unverified rumors about batchmates or faculty.

4. Ignore Digital Footprints: Don't assume "deleted" posts are gone forever; archival tools and screenshots can make them permanent.

5. Post During Lectures: Avoid using social media during class time to maintain focus on your "literate engineering" journey.
